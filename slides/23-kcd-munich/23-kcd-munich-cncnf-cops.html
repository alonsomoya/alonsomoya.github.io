The rules of the game

- So in the mids of this full-scale engineering transformation and in view of the challenges ahead, 1A went to create what we call the engineering community framework - Which state the sort of rules of the game for communities within our company - Which in my opinion is a great first step in the inception of communities of practice in your own organization

Different from traditional schemas

Within the framework we get to state that CoPs differ from tradional schemas in that they are - Bottom-up decision making structures - Collective and continous learning - Participant and member driven - And problem/solution focused You cannot image how many times I have shown this slide to managers, so it's a very useful one

Common priorities

- And common priorities across communities - With the main one towards sharing knowledge and best practice - Followed by creating sense of belonging, in a shared area with no stupid questions - Networking - Collaborating - Standarizing - Influencing Decision Making: a powerful but often overlooked one - And collective innovation

Vibrant Ecosystem

- Once the framework was there - We have seen a surge of a very vibrant ecosystem with more than 110 communities - From language oriented ones, such as go/python, some practical ones such as MyOS for those of us who decide to have a different OS than windows - To Green IT, whose ambassador is somewhere in the back of this room
